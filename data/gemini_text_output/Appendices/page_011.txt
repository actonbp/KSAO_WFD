**POSSIBLE LONG-TERM HEALTH EFFECTS**
» Physical effects: Collapsed veins and bacterial infections of the blood vessels and heart valves; Liver
disease, especially in users who also abuse alcohol or have pre-existing liver conditions.
» Mental health disorders: Depression and antisocial personality disorder.

**OTHER HEALTH-RELATED ISSUES**
» Pregnancy: Heroin use during pregnancy can lead to miscarriage, premature birth, and neonatal
abstinence syndrome.
» Infectious diseases: Increased risk of HIV, hepatitis B and C, and other blood-borne viruses from needle
sharing.

**WITHDRAWAL SYMPTOMS**
» Physical symptoms: Nausea, vomiting, muscle cramps, diarrhea, sweating, tremors, and aching muscles.
» Psychological symptoms: Intense craving for the drug, anxiety, insomnia, depression, and irritability.

**TREATMENT OPTIONS WITH MEDICATION**
» Methadone: A long-acting opioid used in maintenance therapy to prevent withdrawal symptoms and
reduce cravings.
» Buprenorphine (Suboxone, Subutex): Reduces symptoms of opioid dependence and withdrawal and can
block the effects of other opioids.
» Naltrexone: Blocks opioid receptors and prevents opioids from having an effect.

**PRESCRIPTION OPIOIDS**

**Commercial/Street Names**—Prescription opioids include a variety of drugs, such as Oxycodone (OxyContin,
Percocet), Hydrocodone (Vicodin, Norco), Morphine (Kadian, Avinza), and Fentanyl, among others. There are
many street names, depending upon the medication type.
» Oxycodone: O.C., Oxycet, Oxycotton, Oxy, Hillbilly Heroin, Percs
» Hydrocodone: Vike, Watson-387
» Morphine: M, Miss Emma, Monkey, White Stuff
» Hydromorphone, Dilaudid: D, Dillies, Footballs, Juice, Smack
» Methadone, Dolophine, and Methadose: Amidone, Fizzies With MDMA, Chocolate Chip Cookies
» Codeine: Captain Cody, Cody, Lean, Schoolboy, Sizzurp, Doors & Fours, Loads, Pancakes and Syrup
» Fentanyl and Carfentanil: Apache, China Girl, China White, Dance Fever, Friend, Goodfella, Gray Death,
Jackpot, Murder 8, TNT, and Tango and Cash.

**COMMON FORMS**
» Tablets and capsules: The most common form for oral ingestion.
» Liquids and syrups: For oral use where swallowing pills is difficult.
» Patches: For transdermal delivery of medication, particularly fentanyl.
» Suppositories: Rectal administration.

**COMMON WAYS TAKEN**
» Oral ingestion: Pills or liquids are taken by mouth.
» Snorting: Crushing pills to snort the powder.
» Injecting: Dissolving powders for injection, which increases risk for more severe health effects.
» Transdermal: Applying patches to the skin.

**POSSIBLE SHORT-TERM HEALTH EFFECTS**
» Psychological effects: Euphoria, feeling of relaxation.
» Physical effects: Pain relief, drowsiness, nausea, constipation, slowed breathing.
» Dangerous effects: Overdose can lead to profound respiratory depression, coma, and death.
