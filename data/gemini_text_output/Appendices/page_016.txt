**COMMON WAYS TAKEN**
» Sniffing or snorting: From containers or directly from a product.
» Bagging: Inhaling fumes from substances sprayed or deposited inside a plastic or paper bag.
» Huffing: From a rag soaked in a substance and then held to the face or stuffed in the mouth.
» Inhaling from balloons: Filled with nitrous oxide.

**POSSIBLE SHORT-TERM HEALTH EFFECTS**
» Psychological effects: Euphoria, hallucinations, and impaired judgment.
» Physical effects: Dizziness, slurred speech, uncoordinated movements, and lethargy.
» Dangerous effects: Sudden sniffing death syndrome from heart failure, asphyxiation, suffocation,
   convulsions, and comas.

**POSSIBLE LONG-TERM HEALTH EFFECTS**
» Neurological damage: Including brain damage that may result in cognitive impairments and motor
   coordination issues.
» Liver and kidney damage: From chronic exposure to certain chemicals.
» Hearing loss: Particularly from solvents.
» Bone marrow damage: From benzene and other solvents.
» Cardiac failure: Some inhalants can sensitize the heart to adrenaline, leading to arrhythmias and potential
   heart failure.

**OTHER HEALTH-RELATED ISSUES**
» Pregnancy: Low birth weight, bone problems, delayed behavioral development due to brain problems,
   altered metabolism and body composition.
» Psychological dependence: With cravings and compulsive use behavior.

**WITHDRAWAL SYMPTOMS**
» Physical Effects: Nausea, loss of appetite, sweating, tremors, and problems sleeping.
» Mood changes: Including irritability and depression.

**TREATMENT OPTIONS WITH MEDICATION**
No specific medications are approved to treat inhalant addiction.

**STEROIDS (ANABOLIC)**
**Commercial/Street Names**—Anabolic steroids, used both legally and illegally, are known by various commercial
names. Currently, there are more than 100 different types of anabolic steroids that have been developed; some
common ones are Anadrol, Proviron, Dianabol, Winstrol, Deca-Durabolin, Equipoise, Oxandrin, and Halotestin.
Street names include arnolds, gym candy, pumpers, roids, stackers, weight trainers, and juice.

**COMMON FORMS**
» Oral pills: Easy to use but can have significant effects on the liver.
» Injectable solutions: Typically injected into muscles.
» Gels or creams: Applied topically for local or systemic effect.
» Patches: Deliver a steady dose through the skin.

**COMMON WAYS TAKEN**
» Oral ingestion: Pills are swallowed.
» Injection: Steroids are injected directly into muscles.
» Topical application: Gels and creams are applied to the skin.
» Transdermal patches: Patches are attached to the skin for a slow release of the drug.
» Often used in specific phases:
   › Cycling: Steroids take in cycles of 6 to 12 weeks (known as the “on” period), followed by 4 weeks to
     several months off.
