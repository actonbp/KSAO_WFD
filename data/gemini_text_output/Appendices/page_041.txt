APPENDICES 435

**APPENDIX F:
SITES CONTAINING INFORMATION
ON EVIDENCE-BASED SCREENING,
ASSESSMENT AND TREATMENTS FOR
BEHAVIORAL HEALTH CONDITIONS**

Many reputable websites provide comprehensive information on evidence-based treatment practices for
substance use and mental health disorders. These resources are widely used for up-to-date and scientifically
supported information.

1.  **National Institute on Drug Abuse (NIDA):** NIDA's website offers extensive resources on drug abuse,
    addiction, and treatment methods based on scientific research. It's an excellent source for understanding
    the science of drug abuse and addiction, medication assisted treatments, and the principles of evidence-
    based treatment.
    ‣ Website: https://www.drugabuse.gov

2.  **Substance Abuse and Mental Health Services Administration (SAMHSA):** SAMHSA provides information
    on both substance use and mental health disorders. It offers a treatment locator, publications, data, and
    guidelines for evidence-based practices. SAMHSA is a critical resource for finding treatment facilities and
    understanding the latest research and policies.
    ‣ Website: https://www.samhsa.gov
    ‣ Website for SAMHSA Evidence-based Practices Resource Center:
      https://www.samhsa.gov/resource-search/ebp

3.  **Addiction Technology Transfer Center Network: The ATTC Network is an international, multidisciplinary
    resource for professionals in the substance use disorder treatment and recovery services field.** Funded
    by SAMHSA, the ATTC Network includes 10 Regional Centers, the District of Columbia, international
    centers (Puerto Rico, the U.S. Virgin Islands, and the Pacific Islands of Guam, American Samoa, Palau, the
    Marshall Islands, Micronesia, and the Mariana Islands), and a Network Coordinating Office.
    ‣ Website: https://attcnetwork.org/

4.  **Mental Health Technology Transfer Center Network: The MHTTC Network is a multidisciplinary
    resource for professionals in the mental health treatment and recovery services field.** Funded by the
    Substance Abuse and Mental Health Services Administration (SAMHSA), the MHTTC Network includes 10
    Regional Centers and a Network Coordinating Office.
    ‣ Website: https://mhttcnetwork.org/

5.  **Cochrane Library:** Cochrane is known for its high-quality systematic reviews and meta-analyses on
    healthcare interventions, including those related to substance use and mental health. It's a valuable
    resource for evidence-based treatment practices.
    ‣ Website: https://www.cochranelibrary.com

6.  **Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC):** The CDC provides information on public health,
    including substance use and mental health. Its resources can be useful for understanding broader health
    implications and evidence-based approaches to prevention and treatment.
    ‣ Website: https://www.cdc.gov
