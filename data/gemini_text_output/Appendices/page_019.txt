**WITHDRAWAL SYMPTOMS**
» Physical symptoms: Headaches, increased appetite, and fatigue.
» Psychological symptoms: Irritability, anxiety, depression, and strong cravings for nicotine.

**TREATMENT OPTIONS WITH MEDICATION**
» Nicotine replacement therapy (NRT): Includes patches, gum, lozenges, inhalers, or nasal sprays that deliver small amounts of nicotine to ease withdrawal symptoms.
» Bupropion (Zyban): An antidepressant that also helps reduce nicotine cravings and withdrawal symptoms.
» Varenicline (Chantix): Blocks nicotine receptors in the brain, reducing pleasure from smoking and easing withdrawal symptoms.

**ALCOHOL**

**Commercial Names**—Alcohol is marketed under countless brand names depending on the type of alcoholic beverage. Common types include beer, wine, spirits (such as vodka, whiskey, rum, and gin), and liqueurs.

**COMMON FORMS**
» Beer: Typically brewed from barley and hops.
» Wine: Made from fermented grapes or other fruits.
» Spirits: Distilled beverages with a higher alcohol content than beer or wine.
» Liqueurs: Sweetened spirits with various flavors and added ingredients.

**COMMON WAYS TAKEN**
» Oral ingestion: Consumed in liquid form as a beverage.

**POSSIBLE SHORT-TERM HEALTH EFFECTS**
» Psychological effects: Euphoria, lowered inhibitions, and relaxation, progressing to confusion and mood swings with higher consumption.
» Physical effects: Slurred speech, motor impairment, and drowsiness, progressing to vomiting, blackouts, and alcohol poisoning at high levels.
» Dangerous effects: Risk of injuries due to impaired judgment and coordination, respiratory depression, and potential fatal alcohol poisoning.

**POSSIBLE LONG-TERM HEALTH EFFECTS**
» Liver diseases: Including fatty liver, hepatitis, fibrosis, and cirrhosis.
» Cardiovascular problems: High blood pressure, heart disease, and stroke.
» Cancer: Increased risk of developing several types of cancer, including breast, mouth, throat, esophagus, liver, and colon.
» Neurological damage: Including peripheral neuropathy and dementia.

**OTHER HEALTH-RELATED ISSUES**
» Mental health: Depression, anxiety, and other mental health problems can worsen with heavy drinking.
» Pregnancy: Alcohol use during pregnancy can lead to fetal alcohol spectrum disorders (FASDs).

**WITHDRAWAL SYMPTOMS**
» Mild symptoms: Anxiety, shaky hands, headache, nausea, vomiting, and insomnia.
» Severe symptoms: Confusion, hallucinations, seizures, and delirium tremens (DTs).

**TREATMENT OPTIONS WITH MEDICATION**
» Disulfiram (Antabuse): Discourages drinking by causing unpleasant effects such as nausea and flushing when alcohol is consumed.
» Naltrexone: Reduces the pleasurable effects of alcohol, thus reducing craving.
» Acamprosate (Campral): Helps stabilize the brain chemistry that is disrupted by alcoholism.
